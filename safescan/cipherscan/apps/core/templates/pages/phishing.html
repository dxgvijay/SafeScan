How to identify phishing emails

Five essential tips to spot malicious emails before they cause harm.

Check the sender address

Hover over the display name. If the email address looks suspicious or doesn't match the domain, it's a red flag.

Hover before you click

Always hover over links to preview the actual URL before clicking. Look for misspellings like "rnicrosoft" vs "microsoft".

Watch for urgency & threats

Phishing emails often demand immediate action â€” "Your account will be suspended!" Legitimate companies don't do this.

Don't open unexpected attachments

Invoices, voicemails, and shipping notices you didn't expect are common phishing lures. Verify with the sender first.

Look for poor grammar & design

Typos, awkward phrasing, mismatched logos, and low-quality graphics are strong indicators of a phishing attempt.
